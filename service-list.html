Duration: 30mins

Here are some general steps you can expect:

Consultation: Your stylist or barber will typically begin by asking you about your desired haircut style, as well as any concerns or preferences you may have. They may also assess your hair type and condition to determine the best approach for your haircut.

Hair preparation: Next, your hair will be washed and towel-dried, or sprayed with water to dampen it before cutting. This helps to remove any dirt or product buildup and makes it easier to cut and style.

Cutting: Your stylist or barber will then use scissors or clippers to trim your hair to the desired length and shape. They may also use thinning shears or a razor to create texture or remove bulk from your hair.

Styling: After the cutting is complete, your stylist or barber may apply styling products such as gel, mousse, or hairspray to your hair to achieve your desired look. They may also blow dry your hair or use hot tools such as straighteners or curling irons to finish the style.

Finishing touches: Once your haircut is complete, your stylist or barber will typically brush or comb your hair and make any final adjustments to ensure that it looks polished and even.

Payment: Finally, you will pay for your haircut, and may also leave a tip for your stylist or barber if you're satisfied with the results.

Cash: Cash is a common payment method for beauty services, especially for smaller businesses or individual beauty professionals. It's important to bring the correct amount of cash to pay for your services, as not all beauty professionals may be able to provide change.

Credit or Debit Card: Many salons and beauty professionals now accept credit or debit cards for payment. This method is convenient, as you don't need to have cash on hand, and it also allows you to earn rewards or cash back on your purchases. Be sure to check with your salon or beauty professional to see if they accept card payments and what types of cards they accept.

Mobile Payment: Mobile payment options such as Apple Pay, Google Pay, or Venmo may also be accepted by some salons and beauty professionals. This method is convenient if you don't want to carry cash or cards with you, and it allows for contactless payment.

Online Payment: Some beauty professionals may offer online payment options through their website or booking platform. This method is convenient for pre-paying for services or for virtual consultations.
